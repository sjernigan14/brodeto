Raleigh Iron Works & Beyond: A Neighborhood Dining Guide

Published by Brodeto | December 2025

Raleigh Iron Works is an 18-acre mixed-use development built on the historic Peden Steel facility, blending industrial character with modern dining, retail, and community spaces. Brodeto is proud to call it home. This thriving district has become a culinary destination in its own right, and it sits at the heart of some of Raleigh's most vibrant dining neighborhoods. Whether you're planning a special evening, exploring new flavors, or seeking the perfect dining experience, this guide will help you navigate the food scene around Raleigh Iron Works and discover neighborhood favorites worth discovering.

Raleigh Iron Works

Brodeto anchors the food scene at Raleigh Iron Works alongside neighborhood dining neighbors like Ponysaurus Brewing—the Durham-based brewery's second location—Eastcut Sandwich Bar, and Andia's Ice Cream. The development also features the striking Collision Project public art installations, which have become Instagram-worthy landmarks for visitors and locals alike. Complimentary deck parking makes it easy to explore multiple venues, or simply settle in for a long dinner at Brodeto and enjoy the vibrant atmosphere of the Iron Works district. Visit Brodeto to reserve your table for an evening of authentic Adriatic seafood and exceptional wine service.

Five Points

Just minutes from Iron Works lies Five Points, one of Raleigh's most historic and walkable neighborhoods. This charming district offers a diverse dining scene that complements the food-focused atmosphere of Iron Works. Bloomsbury Bistro delivers chef-driven cuisine perfect for memorable date nights, while NOFO @ The Pig offers Southern comfort food in a converted Piggly Wiggly storefront—a true neighborhood gem. For craft beer enthusiasts, Crafty provides a curated selection and relaxed atmosphere. Five Points is also home to independent shops, galleries, and galleries, making it ideal for an extended evening of dining and exploration.

North Hills & Midtown

A quick 5-minute drive from Iron Works puts you in North Hills, Raleigh's upscale mixed-use hub and a prime destination for travelers. The area is anchored by three major hotels—the Renaissance Raleigh North Hills Hotel, AC Hotel Raleigh North Hills, and Hilton Raleigh North Hills—making it a natural gathering place for out-of-town guests. Many visitors to Brodeto choose to stay in the area and explore North Hills' diverse dining options. While Ruth's Chris and STIR offer excellent dining experiences, Brodeto stands out as the standout culinary choice worth a short drive from North Hills, especially for those seeking authentic Adriatic seafood and sophisticated wine pairings that enhance a corporate dinner or special celebration.

The Crawford Hospitality Trail

For a comprehensive Raleigh dining experience, explore the Crawford Hospitality family of restaurants. Build a multi-night culinary tour starting with Jolie's refined French bistro, Crawford & Son for contemporary New Southern cuisine, Bar Sousterre for underground cocktail experiences, Crawford Brothers for premium steakhouse dining, and Brodeto for Adriatic seafood excellence. Each restaurant reflects a distinct culinary philosophy while maintaining the same commitment to ingredient quality and technical precision. Crawford Hospitality's full portfolio offers Raleigh visitors and locals a distinctive range of dining experiences, each worth multiple visits.
