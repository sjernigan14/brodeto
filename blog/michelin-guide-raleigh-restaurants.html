Brodeto in the MICHELIN Guide 2025

Recognition from the world's most respected culinary authority

In early 2025, the MICHELIN Guide announced its first official recognition of Brodeto: a place in the MICHELIN Guide 2025 as a Recommended restaurant. For those familiar with the global culinary landscape, this represents a significant threshold. The MICHELIN Guide's Recommended designation is reserved for restaurants that meet the Guide's rigorous standards for quality, cleanliness, and the kitchen's skill level. It's an external validation of what we've known since Brodeto opened: that this restaurant represents serious, accomplished cooking.

Understanding the MICHELIN Guide

For nearly 130 years, the MICHELIN Guide has been the world's benchmark for restaurant excellence. It began as a promotional tool to encourage automobile travel in France. Today it's the most influential restaurant rating system in the world, respected by chefs and diners across continents. The Guide publishes annually in selected cities and regions, and inclusion is never guaranteed. Inspectors visit unannounced, assess the restaurant against specific criteria, and make determinations about whether a restaurant merits recognition.

The MICHELIN system uses a three-tier star designation. One star indicates "a very good restaurant in its category." Two stars means "excellent cuisine, worth a detour." Three stars is "exceptional cuisine, worth a special journey." For a newly opened restaurant like Brodeto, MICHELIN recognition at any level in the first year represents a significant acknowledgment of quality and consistency.
